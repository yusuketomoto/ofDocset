286 /*
287  * Y2K compliance in libpng:
288  * =========================
289  *
290  * January 6, 2011
291  *
292  * Since the PNG Development group is an ad-hoc body, we can't make
293  * an official declaration.
294  *
295  * This is your unofficial assurance that libpng from version 0.71 and
296  * upward through 1.5.0 are Y2K compliant. It is my belief that
297  * earlier versions were also Y2K compliant.
298  *
299  * Libpng only has three year fields. One is a 2-byte unsigned integer
300  * that will hold years up to 65535. The other two hold the date in text
301  * format, and will hold years up to 9999.
302  *
303  * The integer is
304  * "png_uint_16 year" in png_time_struct.
305  *
306  * The strings are
307  * "png_charp time_buffer" in png_struct and
308  * "near_time_buffer", which is a local character string in png.c.
309  *
310  * There are seven time-related functions:
311  * png.c: png_convert_to_rfc_1123() in png.c
312  * (formerly png_convert_to_rfc_1152() in error)
313  * png_convert_from_struct_tm() in pngwrite.c, called in pngwrite.c
314  * png_convert_from_time_t() in pngwrite.c
315  * png_get_tIME() in pngget.c
316  * png_handle_tIME() in pngrutil.c, called in pngread.c
317  * png_set_tIME() in pngset.c
318  * png_write_tIME() in pngwutil.c, called in pngwrite.c
319  *
320  * All handle dates properly in a Y2K environment. The
321  * png_convert_from_time_t() function calls gmtime() to convert from system
322  * clock time, which returns (year - 1900), which we properly convert to
323  * the full 4-digit year. There is a possibility that applications using
324  * libpng are not passing 4-digit years into the png_convert_to_rfc_1123()
325  * function, or that they are incorrectly passing only a 2-digit year
326  * instead of "year - 1900" into the png_convert_from_struct_tm() function,
327  * but this is not under our control. The libpng documentation has always
328  * stated that it works with 4-digit years, and the APIs have been
329  * documented as such.
330  *
331  * The tIME chunk itself is also Y2K compliant. It uses a 2-byte unsigned
332  * integer to hold the year, and can hold years as large as 65535.
333  *
334  * zlib, upon which libpng depends, is also Y2K compliant. It contains
335  * no date-related code.
336  *
337  * Glenn Randers-Pehrson
338  * libpng maintainer
339  * PNG Development Group
340  */
